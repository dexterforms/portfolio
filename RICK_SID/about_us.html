About Us

SID( Umer Siddiqui )

Hi, my name is Umer, but most of my friends call me Sid. I was born in Karachi, Pakistan and have been a technophile for as long as I can remember. I first got my hands dirty with web design in 2007 and never looked back. I’ll be the first to admit that I am horrible at drawing (I’m pretty sure my niece’s stick figures are better than mine) but what I adore about web design is that it provides me a wayto drop my imagination into 5th gear and really spill it onto a page. Much to the chagrin of my wife (and sometimes my sleep schedule) I’m a visual perfectionist, but I wouldn’t have it any other way.


I know web design is my life’s calling because it’s a constantly evolving art form. It’s like technology with personality. I’m not interested in one-and-done assignments or just making carbon copy web sites. My goal is to engineer something that really speaks to its audience and its owners, something unique and vibrant, something that other companies and designers will look at and say, “Now that is a cool site.”


Along the same lines, my mission with rickandsid.com is to cultivate relationships with clients that never stop growing. I welcome anyone who has worked with us in the past to contact me any time for some friendly guidance and support, especially when expanding the scope or function of a site. My projects are my babies, and I love to watch them grow up!

Rick( Richard Wheeler )

I’ve worked in marketing across a ton of industries, from public relations to grant writing to SEO and SEM companies, and have a few years’ experience in freelance blogging and copywriting. I’m a writer first and foremost and I help companies boil down their sales messages into succinct, attractive, and most importantly, sales-increasing copy. From there, I help publish that content in places it will get noticed, whether it’s a web site’s splash page, their blog, or their social media arm. My decade of experience has taught me that persuasive, engaging content requires more than just being a talented writer – a good copywriter understands that explaining a product or service then dropping a call to action at the end (e.g., “Buy now! Call us today!”) just doesn’t get the job done. There’s an entire sphere of influences to consider – both internal and external – that play out as you try to make a sale online.


There’s definitely a method to my madness. I hold a master’s degree in organizational psychology and know how to pepper in certain cognitive triggers that guide people through the decision-making process (hint:three of them should be in the first sentence of your web page). I also have a great eye for design and work closely with Sid in drawing up mockups and crafting the aesthetics of sites. This goes beyond just color schemes and graphics;a good layout considers how the eye naturally travels around the page and how the typical user’s experience and goals coincide with the site’s navigation. It’s interplay between science and art, and if you’re ready to see how it can add that extra oomph to your business, drop us a line.
